Recycling programs help cities reduce landfill waste by collecting plastics, paper, and glass and converting them to new materials. Municipal collection schedules vary by city.
Electric buses reduce urban air pollution and can lower operational costs over time, but upfront costs and charging infrastructure are barriers.
Water conservation includes measures like low-flow fixtures, drought-resistant landscaping, and efficient irrigation systems for public parks.
Community gardens increase local food access, strengthen neighborhood ties, and can be supported by volunteer days and small municipal grants.
Public Wi-Fi in city centers increases access but raises questions about bandwidth management and privacy safeguards.
